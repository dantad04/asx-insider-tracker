What is ASX Insider Tracker?

ASX Insider Tracker monitors director share transactions on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX). Under ASX Listing Rule 3.19A, every director of a listed company must lodge an Appendix 3Y form within five business days of any change in their relevant interests. This tracker collects, parses, and analyses those filings to surface patterns in insider trading activity and flag late lodgements.

The project is built as a portfolio piece demonstrating end-to-end data engineering: automated PDF parsing, a relational database, a FastAPI backend, and a JavaScript frontend — all containerised with Docker.

Step 1 — ASX Announcement Feed

The ASX provides a public JSON feed of company announcements. The tracker queries this feed and filters for documents with type 3Y (Appendix 3Y — Change in Director's Interest Notice). Each matching announcement yields a URL pointing to a PDF.

Step 2 — PDF Parsing

Appendix 3Y forms are unstructured PDFs with no consistent layout. The parser uses pdfplumber to extract text, then applies a set of regex patterns tuned to the most common ASX form templates. Key fields extracted:

  • Date of change — when the director's interest changed
  • Date lodged — when the form was submitted to ASX
  • Nature of change — on-market buy/sell, off-market transfer, options exercise
  • Quantity — number of securities transacted
  • Price per security — where disclosed
  • Director name & company ticker

The parser includes fallback patterns and OCR-error tolerance to handle scanned or low-quality PDFs.

Step 3 — Compliance Classification

Once a trade is stored, the system calculates the gap (in business days, Mon–Fri) between the date of change and the date lodged. Under ASX Listing Rule 3.19A.2, directors have five business days to lodge. Any filing beyond that threshold is classified as late:

ClassificationBusiness Days to Lodge
Compliant1 – 5 business days
Minor6 – 10 business days (1–5 days past deadline)
Moderate11 – 20 business days (6–15 days past deadline)
Severe21+ business days (16+ days past deadline)

Data Sources

ASX Company Announcements Platform

Primary source for all filings. The ASX publicly exposes an announcement feed at asx.com.au which returns JSON metadata and PDF links for each lodgement. Appendix 3Y forms are identified by document type 3Y.

Coverage: All ASX-listed companies. Historical seed data covers approximately 2024–2025; live scraping covers 2026 onwards.

Historical Seed Data (JSON)

A bulk JSON export of ~14,400 historical director transaction records (sourced from a prior ASX data aggregation) was imported to bootstrap the database. Important: dates in the seed data are less reliable than dates extracted directly from 3Y PDFs — compliance violations are therefore only reported for trades where the date of lodgement is on or after 1 January 2026 (i.e. verified via direct PDF parsing).

Price Data — Yahoo Finance Blocked

The tracker is designed to fetch daily OHLCV price data for each company to calculate price performance around insider trade dates. Yahoo Finance's API currently blocks automated requests from this environment. The price_snapshots table exists but is empty. A future version will use an alternative data provider.

Update Frequency

Current status: Manual

The pipeline is triggered manually. Each run queries the ASX announcement feed for new 3Y filings, downloads any PDFs not yet in the database, parses them, and upserts records.

Planned: A daily scheduled job (cron or Celery beat) will run at approximately 7 pm AEDT each trading day, shortly after the ASX closes at 4 pm and the announcement platform is updated.

Known Limitations

⚠️
PDF parsing accuracy — Appendix 3Y forms have no standard machine-readable format. Some PDFs use scanned images, unusual layouts, or proprietary fonts. The parser achieves high accuracy on common templates but may miss or misread fields on atypical forms. Failed PDFs are logged for manual review.
⚠️
Historical date reliability — Pre-2026 trade dates come from the seed JSON export, not from PDFs directly. Some records appear to use the announcement date rather than the actual date of change, which would inflate calculated lodgement delays. Compliance violations are therefore restricted to post-2025 verified data only.
⚠️
No price data — Without price history, the "smart money score" (price performance in the 30 days following a director buy) cannot yet be calculated. This feature is planned once a reliable price data source is integrated.
⚠️
Public holidays not excluded from business day counts — The compliance calculation uses Mon–Fri weekday counts via numpy.busday_count but does not account for Australian public holidays. This may slightly overstate days-late figures for trades around public holiday periods.
ℹ️
Not a complete record — The tracker only processes Appendix 3Y forms. Changes disclosed via other mechanism (e.g. substantial holder notices, initial director interests) are not included.
