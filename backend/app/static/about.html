About ASX Insider Tracker

ASX Insider Tracker monitors director trading activity across every company listed on the Australian Securities Exchange. It automatically scrapes, parses, and analyses Appendix 3Y filings — the forms directors must lodge when they buy or sell shares in their own company. The goal: surface the trades that matter, flag potential compliance issues, and help investors, journalists, and researchers follow the smart money.

How It Works

Every day, the system pulls the latest Appendix 3Y filings from the ASX. Each PDF is parsed to extract the director's name, the company, the trade date, number of shares, price, and whether it was an on-market transaction. These trades are stored in a PostgreSQL database alongside the full universe of ASX-listed companies. The system currently tracks 15,000+ trades across 1,500+ companies and 4,200+ directors.

Key Features

Smart Money Scores

Each director receives a numerical score from 0 to 10 based on how often their trades preceded favourable price movements, weighted for recency. Recent trades carry more weight than old ones using exponential decay with an 18-month half-life — so a director who called three trades correctly last year outscores one whose last accurate trade was five years ago. A score of 8 or above means the director has a strong recent track record of buying before prices rise.

Compliance Monitoring

The tracker flags directors who appear to have filed their Appendix 3Y late — the statutory deadline under ASX Listing Rule 3.19A.2 is five business days after a notifiable change. Potential violations are classified as minor (6–10 business days), moderate (11–20), or severe (21+). Moderate and severe cases are cross-checked against the original ASX PDF to filter out false positives caused by date mismatches in the source data.

Sector Heatmap

See which industry sectors are experiencing the most insider buying or selling activity over the last 90 days. Each sector is classified as bullish, bearish, or neutral based on the ratio of buy value to sell value across all director trades in that sector.

Date-Range Filtering

Drill into any time period to see which directors were active and how accurate their trades were within that specific window. Smart Money Scores can be filtered to a custom date range and compared side-by-side with each director's all-time score, making it easy to identify directors who were particularly prescient during a specific market event or period.

Data Sources

ASX Company Announcements Platform

Primary source for all filings. The ASX publicly exposes an announcement feed at asx.com.au which returns JSON metadata and PDF links for each lodgement. Appendix 3Y forms are identified by document type 3Y.

Coverage: All ASX-listed companies. Historical seed data covers approximately 2024–2025; live scraping covers 2026 onwards.

Historical Seed Data

A bulk JSON export of ~14,400 historical director transaction records was imported to bootstrap the database. Dates in the seed data are less reliable than dates extracted directly from 3Y PDFs — compliance violations are therefore only reported for trades lodged on or after 1 January 2026 (verified via direct PDF parsing).

Update Frequency

The system uses lazy syncing: when a user opens the site, it automatically checks whether more than one hour has passed since the last sync. If so, it fetches the latest filings from the ASX in the background before rendering the page. Users can also trigger an immediate sync at any time using the Refresh Now button on the trade feed.

Known Limitations

⚠️
PDF parsing accuracy — Appendix 3Y forms have no standard machine-readable format. Some PDFs use scanned images, unusual layouts, or proprietary fonts. The parser achieves high accuracy on common templates but may miss or misread fields on atypical forms.
⚠️
Historical date reliability — Pre-2026 trade dates come from the seed JSON export, not directly from PDFs. Some records use the announcement date rather than the actual date of change, which would inflate calculated lodgement delays. Compliance violations are therefore restricted to post-2025 verified data only.
⚠️
Public holidays not excluded — The compliance calculation uses Mon–Fri weekday counts via numpy.busday_count but does not account for Australian public holidays, which may slightly overstate days-late figures around holiday periods.
ℹ️
Appendix 3Y only — The tracker only processes Appendix 3Y forms. Changes disclosed via other mechanisms (substantial holder notices, initial director interest forms) are not included.
